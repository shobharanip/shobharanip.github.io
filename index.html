Shobharani Polasa is an American AI and data leader and machine learning researcher known for her work at the intersection of enterprise AI strategy and applied data science. She focuses on building large-scale intelligent systems that create measurable business value. Polasa earned a Master of Science in Computer Science and Software Engineering from Monmouth University in 2024, where she participated in peer-reviewed research subsequently published in the IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Magazine.[1][2]

Her research introduces the PART (Predictive Athlete Readiness for Tennis) framework — a multimodal weighted ensemble learning system that integrates wearable sensor data, video analysis, physical tests, match statistics, and player surveys to assess athlete readiness and injury risk.[2] The work was featured in a June 2026 press release by Monmouth University's Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering (CSSE).[1]

Education

Polasa completed her Master of Science degree in Computer Science and Software Engineering at Monmouth University in West Long Branch, New Jersey in 2024. At Monmouth she was affiliated with the Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering (CSSE), working alongside faculty members Weihao Qu, Ling Zheng, and Jiacun Wang on applied machine learning research.[1]

Her graduate training spanned machine learning theory, multimodal data systems, ensemble methods, data engineering, and AI systems design. During her programme she co-developed the PART framework, which formed the basis of her IEEE publication.[2]

Career

Polasa has built her professional identity around enterprise AI and data strategy. She focuses on designing end-to-end data intelligence platforms that translate raw organisational data into actionable insights at scale. Her approach is distinguished by a commitment to grounding AI systems in business outcomes rather than technical novelty alone.

"I'm an AI and data leader focused on building systems that create real business value. I think about AI through the lens of strategy and scale."
— Shobharani Polasa

Her professional focus areas include multimodal data fusion, machine learning operations (MLOps), predictive modelling, data architecture, and AI governance. She is particularly interested in systems that integrate heterogeneous data sources — sensors, video, structured records, and survey data — into unified intelligence platforms capable of supporting high-stakes decisions.

Research

Polasa's primary research contribution lies in the domain of sports analytics and multimodal machine learning. Her work demonstrates how ensemble-based AI frameworks can integrate diverse data modalities to produce more accurate and clinically useful predictions than single-modality approaches.

PART Framework

The Predictive Athlete Readiness for Tennis (PART) framework is a multimodal weighted ensemble learning system designed to assess tennis players' readiness, performance potential, and injury risk. PART synthesises five categories of input data:

  • Wearable sensor data — real-time biometric measurements during training and competition
  • Player wellness surveys — subjective self-reported wellness and fatigue indicators
  • Physical performance tests — standardised fitness assessments
  • Match statistics — historical and current match performance data
  • Video analysis — computer vision-derived movement and technique metrics

By combining these modalities through a weighted ensemble strategy, PART produces a composite Athlete Readiness Score and a personalised injury risk assessment for each player. The system is designed to enable coaches and sports medicine staff to intervene proactively before injuries occur.[2]

IEEE Publication

In 2026, Polasa and her colleagues published the paper "Multimodal Injury Risk and Performance Prediction in Tennis Using Weighted Ensemble Learning" in the IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Magazine.[2] The paper was co-authored with Monmouth University faculty Weihao Qu, Ling Zheng, and Jiacun Wang, and fellow alumni Dongyang Wang ('25M) and Francisco E. Alvarez ('25M).[1]

The IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Magazine is published by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), the world's largest technical professional organisation, with over 533,000 members in more than 190 countries.[3]

Recognition

Polasa's research was highlighted by Monmouth University's official communications office in June 2026 as a notable example of CSSE alumni contributing to internationally recognised, peer-reviewed AI research.[1] The publication in the IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Magazine — a peer-reviewed journal of one of the world's foremost technical professional bodies — represents significant recognition in the fields of machine learning and human-centred computing.

The PART framework has potential applications in professional sports organisations, sports medicine programmes, athletic training centres, and preventive healthcare platforms globally. Its multimodal design methodology is also applicable to domains beyond tennis, including other contact and non-contact sports, as well as occupational health monitoring.
