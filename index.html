Shobharani Polasa is an American data scientist, machine learning engineer, and IEEE-published researcher with over 12 years of experience delivering end-to-end predictive modeling, ML engineering, causal inference, and experimentation frameworks across financial services, enterprise, and academic research environments. She is currently a Data Science Consultant at Infosys, serving Bank of America, based in Plano, Texas.

Polasa holds a Master of Science in Data Science & AI (GPA: 3.93/4.00) from Monmouth University (2024) and a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering from Swami Vivekananda Institute of Technology, India. She has co-authored two peer-reviewed publications with IEEE, both focused on multimodal machine learning for sports analytics and injury prediction. She is a member of Upsilon Pi Epsilon (UPE), the International Honor Society for Computing and Information Disciplines.

Research

Polasa's primary research contribution lies in sports analytics and multimodal machine learning. Her work demonstrates how ensemble-based AI frameworks can integrate diverse data modalities to produce more accurate and clinically useful predictions than single-modality approaches.

The PART Framework

During her time at Monmouth University, Polasa co-developed the Predictive Athlete Readiness for Tennis (PART) framework â a multimodal weighted ensemble learning system that integrates five data modalities:

  • Wearable sensors â real-time biometric monitoring
  • Player surveys â subjective wellness and readiness scores
  • Physical performance tests â standardised fitness assessments
  • Match statistics â in-match performance metrics
  • Video analysis â movement quality and technique evaluation

PART produces a composite Athlete Readiness Score and a personalised injury risk assessment for each player, enabling coaches and sports medicine staff to intervene proactively. The system achieved RÂ² = 0.84 and AUC-ROC = 0.79 in validation testing.[2]

IEEE Publications

In 2025, Polasa co-authored "Multimodal Injury Risk Prediction in Tennis", presented at the 2025 IEEE 5th International Conference on Human-Machine Systems (ICHMS).[5] The paper presented a multimodal framework for assessing athlete readiness and predicting injury risk by integrating physiological metrics, wearable data, questionnaires, and video analysis.

In 2026, building on that work, Polasa and colleagues published the expanded journal paper "Multimodal Injury Risk and Performance Prediction in Tennis Using Weighted Ensemble Learning" in the IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics Magazine.[2] Co-authors include Weihao Qu, Ling Zheng, Jiacun Wang (Monmouth University faculty), Dongyang Wang ('25M), and Francisco E.
